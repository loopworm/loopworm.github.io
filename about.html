    We are the farmers of the future.

    A farmed fish, is mostly raised on mostly fish and soybean meal but that is not its natural feed, and the farming of fish has become increasingly expensive and harmful to the environment. The fish usually eat insects in the wild, insects are natural food of the fish, but the farmed fish don’t get access to their natural food. Meanwhile as 200 million indians go to bed hungry every single day, and 80% of Indians face protein deficiency, we are wasting 1/3rd of the food being produced globally. This supposed waste may not be fit for direct human consumption but just imagine if this food waste can be brought back into the loop! We at loopworm are working to turn this vision into a reality. We are farming insects to feed them using food waste which would have otherwise ended up in landfills and thus making natural high protein diet for fisheries and poultry farms.

    Vision

    ‘To produce sustainable food & feed for tomorrow and bring food waste back into the circular loop.’



    Goal

    We are contributing towards United Nation’s 8 Sustainable development goals (SDGs) including Goal number 12: Responsible consumption and production, and Goal number number 13: Climate action.
