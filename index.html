Rising demand

To alleviate people out of hunger and feed growing population, demand of protein is expected to grow by 70% in next 30 years globally. In India situation is terrible with 80% Indians being protein deficient.

Sustainability crises of current feed

Current animal feed either comes from fish meal or from soy meal, unfortunately both these methods are not sustainable due to damage of biodiversity and have faced rising activism lately. The need for an alternate sustainable feed option in the industry is evident.

Rising feed prices

As the oceans are being depleted of fish, catching fish is getting expensive every single day, this directly reflects on the cost farmers have to bear to feed their fish and chickens. Feed constitutes 60-70% of total farming expenditure, therefore rising feed prices directly affect our farmers’ profits and pose a potent threat to their livelihoods

While 80% of Indians face protein deficiency, and 200 million indians go to bed hungry every single day, we are wasting 1/3rd of the food being produced. This supposed waste may not be fit for direct human consumption but just imagine if this food waste can be brought back into the loop!

About us

A farmed fish, is mostly raised on mostly fish and soybean meal but that is not its natural feed, and the farming of fish has become increasingly expensive and harmful to the environment. The fish usually eat insects in the wild, insects are natural food of the fish, but the farmed fish don’t get access to their natural food. Meanwhile as 200 million indians go to bed hungry every single day, and 80% of Indians face protein deficiency, we are wasting 1/3rd of the food being produced globally. This supposed waste may not be fit for direct human consumption but just imagine if this food waste can be brought back into the loop! We at loopworm are working to turn this vision into a reality. We are farming insects to feed them using food waste which would have otherwise ended up in landfills and thus making natural high protein diet for fisheries and poultry farms. We at loopworm visionize ‘To produce sustainable food & feed for tomorrow and bring food waste back into the circular loop.’

LipidoWorm

LipidoWorm is a high-quality purified insect oil that is ideal for fish, poultry, and pet food. Insect oils naturally contain high proportions of medium chain fatty acids and provide an easily digestible source of energy. External studies show additional health benefits and an improved feed intake.

Chitin

Chitin is a structural polysaccharide that forms the constituent of the outer skeleton of insects and crustaceans. A major product derived from Black Soldier fly, it can be converted to Chitosan for uses in pharmaceutics, biomedicine, cosmetics, food industry, and waste-water treatment. The myriad applications are possible because of it's unique properties like bioactivity, biocompatibility, biodegradability and lack of toxicity. The chitin extracted from BSF larvae is superior because of it's low Crystallinity Index and honeycomb like structure, providing better chelating properties for efficient heavy metal ions trapping, necessary for various applications.

FertiWormr

Our fertilizer is composed of natural by-products of the insect breeding industry: food fibers, insect skins and droppings. These components are rich in organic matter, chitin and minerals, which help to restore the natural balance in the soil. Strong in natural calcium, magnesium and nitrogen it is perfect for root development, crop management and landscaping.
